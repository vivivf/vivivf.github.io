Thinking about values

November 21, 2014

This week DBC asked us to reflect about our values and to think about how they can be used to mediate stereotype threat, in case we feel it. The first part of the exercise instructed us to think about times in our life where we have been the happiest, the proudest or the most satisfied and pick which values came to our mind at this moment. Reading the list a lot of important values came to my mind, but I think the one that caught my attention was the "close relationships". When I read "close relationships" I first though about my friends and my family, that are the closest people to me, but relationship is important regardless of how close you are to the person. We live with people, we all depend on people, nobody is self-sufficient, so building and mantaining good relationships is really important for our life. It's important to our personal and professional life. I mentioned in the last week's blog that I have already faced some unpleasant situations at work for being a woman working in the technology area. The good relationships that I have built always helped me in this kind of situation. When you first meet someone and you try to establish a good relationship from the beginning, the person will be more open to interact with you without doing any previous judgement. I've been using this strategy during all my life and it has been working fine most of the times. My time at DBC will not be different. I have already started to build some good relationships in Phase 0, but I'm looking forward to start the Phase 1 and build the relationships in person, which is much more fun :)
