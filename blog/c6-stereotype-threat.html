Stereotype Threads

November 16, 2014

Stereotype threat is an interesting subject to discuss. As a woman who studied electrical engineer and has always worked with technology I can say that being "different" is at least intimidating.

I have faced very unpleasant situations at work only because I'm a woman. No one has to be judged by its race, gender or religion, but this happens all the time.

According to the social psychologist Dr. Claude Steele, the fear of doing something that would confirm a stereotype creates a kind of subconscious self-handicapping. So the black that unconsciously think the white guy is more intelligent than him, will end-up having a worst performance than the white even if he is as much or more qualified as the white guy.

People who are considered vulnerable to stereotype thread have to avoid being affected. So if you are part of this group, as I am, it is really important to be self-confident. If the statistics show that you are in disadvantage, beat them! You are so capable as other people, so don't let anyone say otherwise.
